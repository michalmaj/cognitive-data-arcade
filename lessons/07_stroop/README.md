# Lesson 07 — Stroop Challenge

## Learning Goals

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

1. Explain why the Stroop effect occurs using the dual-route model of information processing and the concept of reading automaticity.
2. Distinguish between the three components of the Stroop effect: interference, facilitation, and the total Stroop effect, and calculate each from raw reaction-time data.
3. Describe how individual differences (age, clinical diagnosis, bilingualism) moderate the size of the Stroop effect.
4. Identify at least two clinical instruments that use Stroop-type tasks and explain what cognitive constructs they assess.
5. Evaluate their own Stroop data against published norms and propose one methodological explanation for any deviation from the 100–300 ms typical range.

## Required Activity

This lesson uses the **Stroop Challenge** game, available in the application as **Lesson 07**. Play at least one full session before attempting the analysis tasks. After playing, access the Stroop Analysis screen (press **Z** from the lesson menu) to retrieve your reaction-time data by condition.

## Prerequisites

- No statistical training is required beyond calculating means and differences.
