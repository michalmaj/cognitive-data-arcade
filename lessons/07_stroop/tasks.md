
## Step 2: Navigate to Lesson 07

Use the arrow keys to navigate to **Lesson 07 — Stroop Challenge** and press **ENTER**. A brief introduction screen will appear. Press **SPACE** or **ENTER** to continue to the lesson menu.

## Step 3: Read the in-app theory slides

Before playing, navigate to the **Theory** section in the lesson menu. Read all five theory slides — they cover automaticity, the three conditions, the Stroop formula, and clinical applications. These slides present the core concepts in condensed form.

## Step 4: Play one full session of Stroop Challenge

Select **Play** from the lesson menu. You will be presented with colour words in coloured ink. Your task is to respond to the **ink colour**, not the word meaning.

- Use the keyboard keys shown on screen to select the ink colour.
- The game alternates between congruent, neutral, and incongruent trials.
- Complete the entire session without stopping. Aim for accuracy, not just speed.

If you finish the session quickly, play a second time — practice effects across sessions are themselves scientifically interesting.

## Step 5: Access the Stroop Analysis screen

Return to the lesson menu and press **Z** (or select **Analysis** if visible). The analysis screen displays your mean reaction time and accuracy for each condition (congruent, neutral, incongruent).

Record the following values in your notebook:

## Step 6: Calculate your Stroop components

Using the values you recorded, calculate the following by hand:

```
Interference     = RT(incongruent) - RT(neutral)         = _____ ms
Facilitation     = RT(neutral)     - RT(congruent)        = _____ ms
Total Stroop effect = RT(incongruent) - RT(congruent)    = _____ ms
```

Compare your total Stroop effect to the published normative range of **100–300 ms** for healthy young adults.

- Is your effect within this range?
- If it is larger: can you think of any within-session factors (fatigue, distraction, reading speed) that might explain it?
- If it is smaller: did you develop a strategy during the session (e.g., deliberately slowing down to avoid errors)?

## Step 7: Examine your accuracy pattern

Look at your accuracy values across conditions. Answer the following:

1. Did your accuracy drop in the incongruent condition compared to the congruent condition? By how much (in percentage points)?
2. If you made errors in the incongruent condition, were they fast errors (response faster than your mean RT) or slow errors (response slower than your mean RT)? A fast error suggests the automatic reading response "won" the competition; a slow error suggests you detected the conflict but still failed to suppress it.

## Step 8: Compare facilitation and interference

You now have two separate quantities: interference and facilitation. Which is larger in your data?

Research consistently finds that interference > facilitation in most participants. If your pattern is reversed (facilitation > interference), consider: is your neutral baseline appropriate? Did you find the neutral stimuli (XXXXX or colour-unrelated words) unexpectedly slow or fast?
