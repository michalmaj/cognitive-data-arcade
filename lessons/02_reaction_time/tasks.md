# Tasks: Lesson 02

## Step 1: Launch the Application

Open a terminal and navigate to the project directory. Run:

```bash
uv run cognitive-data-arcade
```

## Step 2: Open Lesson 02

Press **ENTER** on lesson 02 — **Reaction Time Lab**. The game launches with the Standard preset (60 trials).

> **Note:** If lesson 02 does not yet launch directly from the menu, select lesson 02 from the menu, complete the game, and then return to the main menu.

## Step 3: Play the Reaction Time Lab

Follow the on-screen instructions. Press the indicated key as quickly as possible when the stimulus appears. Complete all 60 trials. After the final trial, press **S** to open the analysis screen.

## Step 4: Interpret Your Histogram

The analysis screen shows a histogram of all recorded reaction times. Examine it carefully and record:

1. **Mean RT** — what is the average, and is it within the typical range for simple RT (180–350 ms)?
2. **Distribution shape** — where is the peak? Is the distribution right-skewed?
3. **Extreme values** — what is the fastest trial? What is the slowest? Is the difference larger or smaller than expected based on the theory?

## Step 5: Discussion Questions

1. **Why is the distribution right-skewed?** Consider what constrains very fast responses from below and what allows slow responses to extend upward without limit.

2. **What would the histogram look like after a sleepless night?** Based on the theory, predict how the shape, mean, and variance would change — not just the mean alone.

3. **Why did Donders consider reaction time a window into the mind?** What does the subtraction method assume, and is that assumption always valid?
